Felis pardina was the scientific name proposed by Coenraad Jacob Temminck in 1827 who described skins of Iberian lynxes that were killed in the area of the Tagus river in Portugal and were traded in Paris and London.[3] It is a monotypic species.[4]

Phylogeny
The Iberian lynx is suggested to have evolved from Lynx issiodorensis.[5][6] Its earliest known fossil remains date to the end of the Early Pleistocene, around one million years ago.[1]

The Iberian lynx genetically diverged as a unique species 1.98 to 0.7 million years ago. Its closest living relative is the Eurasian lynx (Lynx lynx) with which it coexisted (to a certain degree) until the 20th century.
